## Why I Started

I remembered how lost I felt as a junior engineer. Good mentors accelerated my growth tremendously, and I wanted to pay it forward.

## What I Learned About Teaching

**Explaining reveals gaps.** When I couldn't explain something clearly, it meant I didn't understand it well enough myself.

**Everyone learns differently.** Some people need to see code, others need diagrams, others need to talk through problems verbally.

**Ask, don't tell.** Guiding someone to discover the answer themselves is more effective than just giving them the solution.

**Celebrate small wins.** Progress isn't always visible day-to-day, but looking back over months shows tremendous growth.

## The Multiplier Effect

One of my mentees is now mentoring others. Seeing the knowledge and practices spread through the organization has been incredibly fulfilling.
